    HOME REMEDIES

    STEAM INHALATION

    Explanation

    COVID-19 is a respiratory illness. This means that if you contract the virus, it is going to affect the lungs. Every day, we get exposed to different pollutants in the air. We could also be exposed to the virus in the ai if someone around us sneezes or coughs. In this case, keeping the lungs clean all the time is essential.

    Benefits

    Steam inhalation is the best therapy to cleanse the lungs. It adds moisture and warmth in the air, easens any soreness in the throat and dilates blood vessels.

    Procedure

    1. Boil water on the stove for 10 minutes or in an electric heater for a few minutes until there is visible steam.
    2. Optional: Add some turmeric powder, or any essential oils.
    3. Pour the boiling water into a bowl, and place the bowl on a table.
    4. Put a towel over your head and inhale the steam for around 3-5 minutes. Try inhaling and exhaling to get best results.

Additional vitamins and minerals help fight illnesses and give your immune system the boost that it needs to successfully fight off any ailment.

    SLEEP

    Explanation

    Lack of sleep has been associated with many chronic conditions like type-2 diabetes, depression, and more issues in the health of many adults. The average adult needs 7 hours of sleep each night for the best daily well being. By ensuring that you get an adequate amount of sleep each night, you can help give your body the rest it needs to recover and produce important infection fighting cells and antibodies.

    How to get better sleep

    Getting a sufficient amount of sleep can be hard for anyone, especially those with demanding work schedules...we see you college students, but it’s important to get enough sleep for physical and mental health, so here’s some ways to help:

    YOGA-PRANAYAMA

    Explanation

    The lesser number of breaths we inhale per second, the more seconds we are going to live. Pranayama is a section of Yoga that focuses on different types of breathing exercises to control breath.

    Benefits

    Pranayama is an art that teaches you how to control your breath. Inhaling and exhaling for longer periods of time increases the lung capacity, improves mindfulness and controls blood pressure.

    Procedure

    There are many different kinds of pranayama. This will talk about one such called “Sukha Pranayama”.
    1. Inhale from your left nostril for 10 seconds.
    2. Hold your inhaled breath for 6 seconds.
    3. Exhale from your right nostril for 11 seconds.
    4. Hold your breath with no air for 5 seconds.
    5. Repeat this cycle 20 times in 3 rounds for best results.
